Kern Smith (kern.smith@zimperium.com)
2019-02-14 22:32:28

Thanks for adding me @Kiran Patel . For iOS the app and device profile inventory will always come from the MDM , in this case MI Core. As @Anton I correctly said Apple removed the ability for other apps, specifically enterprise apps, to use a private api to locally see the app inventory on the device in iOS 11, which means that you need to sync the app list from an MDM. For Zimperium the sync of apps and profiles can be turned on or off, anatomized, or disassociated from the device / user as part of a privacy configuration based on your requirements.

Kern Smith (kern.smith@zimperium.com)
2019-02-14 22:42:09

On Android, that a bit different than iOS as @Anton I pointed out. As a general rule on Android any app can locally see what other apps are on a device, or in the profile they exist in in the case of Android for Enterprise, with or without an MDM in use. Zimperium does not require the collection or sending of the app data off of the device to do its detections, even for malicious applications, as those are done locally on the device, so again the privacy conversation and configuration really comes back to what will suit your specific requirements. I hope this helped answer some questions.

Hi @Kern Smith thanks for you explanation - coming back to iOS: You say all other detections will still be in place - still means that detections will not work for apps that are not know because of the privacy policy, right? And for Android Enterprise Work Profile deployments (BYOD and COPE): since Mobile@Work is running within the Work Profile, MTD cannot detect malicious apps outside the Work Profile, right? I have heard other MTDs recommend placing the agent outside the Work Profile and others that the MTD agent is not supported within the Work Profile for their solution. Whats your take on this?

Kern Smith (kern.smith@zimperium.com)
2019-02-15 15:29:02

*Thread Reply:* Hi @MichaelM21 That is correct on iOS. Effectively it would match to your existing privacy settings. All other detections, including side-loaded apps will work, so while you may not see the actual app, you can detect if something in an app actually starts to break out and attack your device and info on that device.

For the work profile, Zimperium doesn't require that you run on both sides, as no matter what, you will still see device, and network attacks against the device regardless of their origin, along with any phishing on the work profile side.

Zimperium (and any MTD for that matter) will see only apps on the profile they are installed on, however Zimperium can be installed on both sides of the work container, and enforce that if required by the organization.

As far as recommendations, it all depends on your privacy posture. Some organizations do not want to view anything on the personal side for various reason, including legal restrictions, while some organizations do want to see all of the apps on the device, both on a work and personal side. Zimperium would want to understand your requirements, and then implement the solution in the best way that fits your needs.

Technically the main reason an MTD would want to be on the personal side is to see personal app, and do malicious app detection on the personal side. If their detections are focused mainly on apps, then I can see why they would want to be on the personal side, but that would lead me to really investigate their capabilities on detecting network and device exploitation.

For something to break from the personal side to the work side, where you data is at, they would likely have to exploit the device, and those exploits can come at the device from anywhere, like wifi, or usb chargers as examples, not just apps.
