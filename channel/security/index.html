Hi all, my question is regarding the best practice for password policies in a hybrid device environment (windows, Mac, iOS, android, Linux)

I'm wondering how organisations that have rigid desktop computer password policies view unlocking mobile (Android or iOS) devices, considering that the device may only have a simple pin and yet still contain identical information as the desktop.

How have you approached this difference in security policy?

Paul Conaty (pconaty@cwsi.ie)
2019-12-04 10:16:38

*Thread Reply:* Generally i'd suggest that having brute force protection (max 10 attempts at PIN) 6 digit PIN, timeouts of 1 min or less no grace period make breaking a password on a device very difficult. Add in a container with a separate PIN or MFA (can be done in Android, with MDM, or using MAM apps like Outlook if you want extra security.

Paul Conaty (pconaty@cwsi.ie)
2019-12-04 10:17:00

*Thread Reply:* A good resource for auth discussions is UK NCSC https://www.ncsc.gov.uk/collection/passwords/updating-your-approach

Paul Conaty (pconaty@cwsi.ie)
2019-12-04 10:17:42

*Thread Reply:* MobileIron are also doing some interesting stuff around killing of password auth that might be worth looking at

Paul Conaty (pconaty@cwsi.ie)
2019-12-04 10:19:06

*Thread Reply:* In terms of approaching the conversation i would always take a risk assessment method and try and encourage the client to look at where advice on auth models has moved to as per NCSC link above

Wolfgang Bauer (wolfgang.bauer@lineas.de)
2019-12-04 12:50:34

*Thread Reply:* Paul is right: the pure numbers (length) are nonsense. You need to look into the security level of different authentication methods, attack vectors and what value you want to protect to get an full picture. If its brute force / spray protected, cant be exported, real mandantory secure MFA, conditional acccess validations and/or there is no password but other real good protection you dont need long passwords. There are guidelines from a lot of parties but often outdated or dont mention things as zero password, OTP,... or want to push you in a special direction (marketing). The guidelines and security whitepapers from the vendors and security authorities are good starting points for such discussions, but dont use them unaudited.
